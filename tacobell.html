Taco Bell Wrapped   

December 2022

  • Tableau Dashboard

This idea stemmed from a trend that has been going around on TikTok. Believed to have been originally inspired by Spotify Wrapped, different creators on TikTok are taking data from other areas of their lives (shopping, dining, dating apps, etc.) and creating presentations to show their habits over the last year. I saw a creator post a video on their ‚ÄúChick-fil-A Wrapped‚Äù and instantly knew I should do something similar with my Taco Bell purchase history.

Thus, I moved forward with creating my 2022 Taco Bell Wrapped (or should I say Unwrapped üåÆ).

Data


With some research, it does not appear that Taco Bell has an easy way for users to retrieve their order history. Lucky for me, I use the Taco Bell mobile app for 99.99% of my orders so I receive my order receipts through email. This is not the ideal method, but I went through all these emails from 2022 and recorded information about the date, order total, discount received, items ordered, etc.

Visualization


The data collected limited the complexity of visualization I could create for this project. I opted to use Tableau for this step. Using Canva first, I created the background image/template for my dashboard elements to sit on top of (I thought the crinkled brown paper bag complemented the theme well üòä). Within Tableau, I created KPIs for the reward level, total number of orders, total saved from using my earned rewards, and average total of each order. Then I created a bar graph to see my top menu items in descending order and a line graph to see my order count per month.

Takeaways


This may be sad to say, but I was surprised my total number of orders was not higher. With the frequency I feel like I am ordering Taco Bell, only placing 56 orders in 2022 was surprising (even though this averages at 1.08 times per week). Being in the Fire reward level seems to have paid off as I saved a total of $101.78 this year from using my earned rewards and participating in various challenges. As I expected, my average order total was fairly low at $5.80 as I frequently order from the Cravings Value Menu.

Some of the menu items I ordered most in 2022 were the Nacho Fries, Fiesta Veggie Burrito, Chips and Nacho Cheese, Spicy Potato Soft Taco, and Nachos BellGrande. I was happy to see Nacho Fries were my most ordered item. 37.5% of all orders included this item which is impressive because this item is typically only offered for a limited time basis as the restaurant frequently takes the item on and off of the menu. The top months where I placed the most orders were March and October.
